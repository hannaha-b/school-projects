Overview

Whitney Houston has been honored with numerous awards and accolades recognizing her worldwide success through the music and movie industries. Cited once by the Guinness World Records as the most awarded female artist, Houston has received received over 600 accolades throughout her career.

Legacy

Houston's debut album, Whitney Houston, was the first album by a woman to yield three No. 1 hits on the Billboard music charts.

Houston's second album, Whitney, debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 albums chart, making her the first woman to debut at number one.

Houston was a supporter of Nelson Mandela and the anti-apartheid movement. During her modeling days, she refused to work with agencies who did business with the then-apartheid South Africa.

Houston's rendition of the Star Spangled Banner, the national anthem of the USA, at the 1991 Super Bowl has become the gold standard for anthem solo performances.
